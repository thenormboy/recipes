Espresso (with or without espresso machine)

Description

An espresso is a method in which to brew coffee and can be done with or without an espresso machine. The espresso is shown several times in Part 2 when Joseph is in Italy, and a coffee variation called the Americano which uses espresso shots was even shown when Joseph was in America.

Ingredients

  • 18 to 20g medium to dark roasted beans
  • Filtered Water

Steps using an espresso machine..

  1. Turn on your espresso machine and give it time to warm up.
  2. Once your machine is heated and you're ready to start brewing, place your portafilter on the scale and tare out the weight.
  3. Grind the coffee into your portafilter until you reach your desired weight, 18 to 20g for a double shot.
  4. Even out the bed of coffee in your portafiliter, either through a distribution tool or with your index finger. The goal is to make sure the coffee is level and evenly distributed across the whole basket.
  5. Use your tamper to compress your coffee down with a fair amount of pressure. It's very important that you tamp straight down in order to achieve an even puck. If you tamp at an angle, the extraction of your coffee will be compromised.
  6. Pull your shot and taste your coffee and determine if you need to change any variables to improve the quality of your cup.

Steps without an espresso machine (french press required btw tho..)

  1. Boil one cup of water.
  2. While you wait for the water to heat up, add two tablespoons of freshly, finely ground coffee to your French press.
  3. Add a bit of the boiling water to the French press and stir briefly.
  4. Wait four minutes, then slowly push down the plunger, pausing every few seconds until its all out. Note that it'll be slightly challenging to push the plunger down due to the grind size.
